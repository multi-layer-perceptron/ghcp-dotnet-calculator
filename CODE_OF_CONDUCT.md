## Our Pledge

We pledge to make our community welcoming, safe, and equitable for all.

We are committed to fostering an environment that respects and promotes the
dignity, rights, and contributions of all individuals, regardless of
characteristics including race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical
characteristics, neurodiversity, disability, sex or gender, gender identity or
expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or
social origin, socio-economic position, level of education, or other status.
The same privileges of participation are extended to everyone who participates
in good faith and in accordance with this Covenant.

## Encouraged Behaviors

While acknowledging differences in social norms, we all strive to meet our
community's expectations for positive behavior. We also understand that our
words and actions may be interpreted differently than we intend based on
culture, background, or native language.

With these considerations in mind, we agree to behave mindfully toward each
other and act in ways that center our shared values, including:

1. Respecting the purpose of our community, our activities, and our ways of gathering.
2. Engaging kindly and honestly with others.
3. Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.
4. Taking responsibility for our actions and contributions.
5. Gracefully giving and accepting constructive feedback.
6. Committing to repairing harm when it occurs.
7. Behaving in other ways that promote and sustain the well-being of our community.

## Restricted Behaviors

We agree to restrict the following behaviors in our community. Instances,
threats, and promotion of these behaviors are violations of this Code of Conduct.

1. Harassment, including violating expressed boundaries or continuing unnecessary personal attention after a clear request to stop.
2. Character attacks, including insulting, demeaning, or pejorative comments directed at a community member or group.
3. Stereotyping or discrimination based on identities or traits.
4. Sexualization that is inappropriate for the context or purpose of the community.
5. Violating confidentiality by sharing or acting on personal or private information without permission.
6. Endangerment, including causing, encouraging, or threatening violence or other harm.
7. Other behavior that threatens the well-being of our community.

Additional restricted behavior includes:

1. Impersonating another person or using a misleading identity to evade enforcement.
2. Failing to credit the sources of contributed content.
3. Sharing promotional or commercial material outside the purpose and norms of the community.
4. Irresponsibly presenting content that includes, links to, or describes restricted behavior.

## Reporting An Issue

Tensions can occur even when community members are trying to collaborate in
good faith. Not every conflict is a Code of Conduct violation, and the
encouraged behaviors above can help prevent conflict and minimize harm.

Report a possible violation privately to the autocloudarc maintainers at
[autocloudarc@outlook.com](mailto:autocloudarc@outlook.com). Do not report
conduct incidents in public Issues, pull requests, or Discussions. Include the
relevant context and evidence, but do not expose another person's private data
more broadly than necessary.

Community moderators will take reports seriously, investigate them fairly, and
prioritize the privacy and security of reporters and affected participants.
Enforcement actions are normally handled privately. Broader communication may
occur when it is necessary for community safety or is part of a mutually agreed resolution.

## Addressing And Repairing Harm

The autocloudarc maintainers adopt the following Contributor Covenant
enforcement ladder as guidance. Moderators may skip a lower level when the
impact or severity of an incident requires a stronger response.

### Warning

For a single incident or series of incidents, moderators may issue a private
written warning explaining the violation and expected correction. Repair may
include acknowledging responsibility, seeking clarification, or making a private apology.

### Temporarily Limited Activities

For repeated behavior or a more serious first incident, moderators may impose a
time-limited cooldown on particular channels, activities, or interactions.
Conditions for return will be communicated privately.

### Temporary Suspension

For repeated violations that warnings have not resolved, or for a single
serious violation, moderators may temporarily suspend participation and set
conditions for return.

### Permanent Ban

For severe harm or a pattern that other responses have not resolved,
moderators may permanently remove access to community spaces and communication channels.

This ladder guides rather than limits moderator judgment. Decisions should be
proportionate, documented, consistent with platform capabilities, and focused
on community safety and repair where repair remains possible.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in repository Issues, pull requests, Discussions,
reviews, workshop interactions, and other project community spaces. It also
applies when someone officially represents this project in public or private spaces.
